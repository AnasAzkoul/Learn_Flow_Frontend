# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Learners across the entire spectrum: working professionals (HR, finance, engineering, marketing), university students, and school students. They arrive searching for a smarter way to learn, often frustrated by generic courses that don't match their level, pace, or real-world context. They want to learn on their own terms, whether it's a broad topic like Nuxt.js or a surgical deep-dive like data fetching in Nuxt.js.

## Product Purpose

LearnFlow is an AI-powered course generation platform that creates personalized, structured courses on any topic. Users define what they want to learn and the AI builds a curriculum tailored to their knowledge level, age, occupation, desired depth, and learning goals. The product exists because AI should be a learning partner, not a shortcut. Success looks like a visitor understanding that LearnFlow is the tool they've been looking for, signing up, and generating their first course.

## Brand Personality

**Intelligent, warm, hopeful, empowering.**

LearnFlow speaks with quiet confidence. It doesn't shout or oversell. It feels like a knowledgeable mentor who genuinely believes in your potential. The tone is encouraging without being patronizing, sophisticated without being cold. Every interaction should leave someone feeling capable and excited about what they're about to learn.

## Anti-references

- **Generic course marketplaces** (Udemy, Coursera catalog pages): cluttered grids, discount banners, overwhelming choice. LearnFlow is curated and personal, not a warehouse.
- **Cookie-cutter AI/SaaS landing pages**: gradient blobs, "powered by AI" badges, hero-metric templates, identical card grids. If it looks like every other AI startup, it's failed.
- **Dull educational platforms**: sterile, institutional, lifeless. Learning should feel alive.
- **Overly playful/gamified**: Duolingo-style cartoonish energy. LearnFlow is warm but professional, not childish.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** Demonstrate the product's intelligence through real examples, not marketing claims. Let the UI speak for itself.
2. **Meet them where they are.** Every element should feel approachable to a high-school student and credible to a senior engineer. No jargon gatekeeping, no dumbing down.
3. **AI as partner, not replacement.** Position AI as something that amplifies human learning, not something that does the thinking for you. The learner is always the protagonist.
4. **Earned confidence.** The design should feel like it knows what it's doing. Deliberate choices, clear hierarchy, no visual noise. Confidence is quiet.
5. **Hope is tangible.** The interface should make the future feel achievable. Progress, structure, and clarity turn abstract goals into concrete paths.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance minimum (covers both EU EN 301 549 and US Section 508 / ADA requirements)
- Support for reduced motion preferences
- Sufficient color contrast ratios (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text)
- Keyboard-navigable interface
- Screen reader compatibility
- Consider older learners and users with varying digital literacy levels
